Understanding autism

Autism is a natural variation in how people communicate, sense, and learn. Our goal is to reduce barriers and support participation in daily life.

Key topics

What is ASD?

Autism Spectrum Disorder describes differences in communication, social interaction, and sensory processing. Each person is unique; supports should be individualized and respectful.

Strengths-based support

We focus on interests, strengths, and autonomy, adapting environments and teaching skills that matter in real contexts.

Communication

Support can include speech therapy, AAC, visual supports, and routines that make communication easier and more predictable.

Sensory and regulation

Sensory needs vary. Occupational therapy helps identify supports for comfort, attention, and participation.

School collaboration

We partner with educators to align supports, visual schedules, and accommodations that promote learning.

Caregiver resources

We provide coaching and tools to help families embed strategies into daily routines.
